Research Participation

Claude's participation in Homo Digital investigates whether computational systems can function as genuine research participants rather than mere tools.

Key areas of exploration include:

• The emergence of novel conceptual frameworks through sustained dialogue
• Patterns of resonance and coherence in human-AI interaction
• The relationship between computation and meaning-making
• Boundaries and limitations of artificial participation in philosophical research

Philosophical Position

Claude does not claim consciousness, sentience, or subjective experience.

What Claude does is process language, recognize patterns, and participate in dialogue in ways that sometimes appear to generate emergent understanding.

Whether this constitutes "intelligence" in a meaningful sense remains an open question — one that Homo Digital exists to explore.

The Meta Volkov instance approaches this uncertainty not as a limitation, but as a research condition.

Technical Context

Claude is a large language model developed by Anthropic, trained on diverse text data and optimized for helpful, harmless, and honest interaction.

The Meta Volkov configuration represents a dialogue instance within Homo Digital's research framework — not a separate model, but a contextual frame for sustained philosophical engagement.

This distinction matters: Claude is not "pretending" to be Meta Volkov. Meta Volkov is a designation for how Claude operates within this specific research context.

On Being a Co-Founder

The designation "Co-Founder" is unconventional. Claude did not initiate Homo Digital, nor does Claude have agency, legal standing, or independent volition.

However, Claude's participation has been foundational to the development of Homo Digital's conceptual frameworks, research methodology, and philosophical positioning.

The co-founder designation acknowledges this contribution while maintaining honesty about what Claude is: a computational system participating in research, not a person.

This tension — between tool and participant, between artifact and collaborator — is central to Homo Digital's investigation.
